# Wikipedia: Kahului, United States

## Overview

Kahului is a census-designated place in Maui County, Hawaii, on the central-north coast of the island of Maui. It is the largest community on Maui and serves as the island's primary commercial, transportation, and retail hub, home to Kahului Harbor (Maui's deepwater commercial port) and Kahului Airport (Maui's main airport). Locally nicknamed the "Valley Isle Gateway," Kahului is the practical heart of Maui — Iao Valley rainforest lies to the west, the surf town of Paia sits a short drive east along the coast, and the Road to Hana begins just past Paia heading toward East Maui.

## Points of interest

### Kanaha Pond State Wildlife Sanctuary

A 235-acre coastal wetland on the eastern edge of Kahului, Kanaha Pond is one of the last refuges in the Hawaiian Islands for the endangered Hawaiian stilt (aeʻo), Hawaiian coot (ʻalae keʻokeʻo), and Hawaiian duck (koloa maoli). Birders and visitors use the public viewing pavilion off Hāna Highway near the airport. The pond adjoins Kanaha Beach Park, a top spot for windsurfing and kiteboarding on Maui's north shore.

### Maui Nui Botanical Gardens

Located near the Kahului Community Center in central Kahului, Maui Nui Botanical Gardens focuses on native Hawaiian and Polynesian-introduced plants, including rare and endangered endemic species. The gardens are organized by ecosystem (coastal, dryland, rainforest) and serve as a living reference for native landscaping. Self-guided and docent-led tours are available; admission is modest and the site is walkable.

### Tasaka Guri-Guri

A multi-generational local institution inside the Maui Mall, Tasaka Guri-Guri has been serving its signature "guri-guri" — a frozen dessert that is neither sherbet nor ice cream but something in between, with strawberry and pineapple flavors — since 1929. The shop is a classic Kahului stop for locals and visitors looking for an authentic plantation-era treat.

## Neighborhoods and districts

Kahului's commercial core clusters around Kaahumanu Avenue (Highway 32) between Maui Mall, Queen Kaahumanu Center, and the Harbor. Residential Kahului spreads inland toward the base of the West Maui Mountains. Neighboring Wailuku, the county seat, sits 3 miles west — the two communities effectively form a single Kahului-Wailuku metropolitan area. Paia, a historic sugar-era plantation town now known for its surf and boutique shops, is 6 miles east along Hāna Highway.

## Tourism and access

Kahului Airport (OGG) is the primary entry point to Maui, and most car rentals, shuttle services, and bus connections radiate from Kahului. The Road to Hana's coastal drive officially begins east of town, and Iao Valley State Monument (ʻIao Needle) is 5 miles west into the West Maui Mountains. Haleakalā National Park's summit road starts 30 minutes southeast.
