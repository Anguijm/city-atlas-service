# Wikipedia: Lexington, United States

## City parks and facilities

Lexington Cemetery's tulip garden. The facility was founded in 1848, during a cholera epidemic.
Lexington has over 100 parks, ranging in size from the 8,719 ft2 (810.0 m2) Smith Street Park to the 659-acre (2.7 km2) Masterson Station Park. Many Lexington parks recently received improvements as part of a $25,183,270.63 investment from the American Rescue Plan Act. Lexington's parks include:

Five public golf courses at Kearney Hill Links, Lakeside, Meadowbrook, Tates Creek, and Picadome
Six dog parks at Jacobson, Masterson Station, Coldstream, Pleasant Ridge, Veteran's Park, and Wellington
Three public 18-hole disc golf courses at Shillito Park, Jacobson Park, and Veterans Park
A public skate park at Woodland Park, featuring 12,000 sq ft (1,100 m2) of "ramps, platforms, bowls, and pipes"
Triangle Park in the heart of downtown Lexington.
Gatton Park on the Town Branch, a $39 million 10-acre (40,000 m2) private park in the center of downtown, opened in August 2025.
